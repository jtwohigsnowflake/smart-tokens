Objection Handling
"Aren't you just adding context?"
Exactly. That is what your model is missing today. But this is not just about adding context. When tokens qualify as Smart Tokens, customers do not just save money on inference. They also get better answers, better performance, stronger governance, security, and auditability because the data, context, and execution stay closer together inside Snowflake.
"We want a standalone context layer that is not locked into Snowflake, so we can keep flexibility across data platforms, models, and applications."
I understand the need for flexibility. You want the freedom to use different models, apps, and platforms without feeling locked in. But for the workflows already using Snowflake data, adding a third-party context layer can create more complexity. Now requests move between the AI agent, model provider, context layer, and data layer. That raises questions around security, governance, auditability, ownership of definitions, and added cost.

Our recommendation is to prove the value first with what you already have. Keep using your AI coding agent or chatbot and leverage Smart Tokens.

You still keep flexibility at the model and application layer, but you avoid fragmenting the data, context, and governance layer that makes enterprise AI trusted.
